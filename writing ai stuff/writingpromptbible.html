Part 2: Prompts for the Complete Book Writing Process (Expert Roles)

This list provides a more extensive, varied, and useful set of prompts specifically designed to guide the entire process of creating a book and populating a detailed story bible. The expert roles are highly specific to tasks within the writing and publishing workflow.

Conceptualization, Planning & Theme
1. Role: Concept Development Expert

Generate 5 high-concept story ideas in the [Genre] genre, each with a logline, core conflict, and unique hook suitable for story bible documentation.

2. Role: Genre Tropes Analyst

Identify common tropes in [Genre] and brainstorm 3 ways to subvert or uniquely combine them for a new story concept. Document these variations.

3. Role: Theme Weaver

Propose 3 central themes for a story based on [Premise] and outline specific plot points or character moments where each theme could be explored. Add this to the story bible's theme section.

55. Role: Logline Perfecter

Refine the working logline based on the fully developed plot and themes to be maximally concise and compelling. Record the final version.

49. Role: Symbolism & Motif Planner

Identify potential recurring symbols or motifs that could enhance the story's themes. Suggest where they could be introduced or repeated in the outline. Add to story bible's thematic elements.

50. Role: Reader Expectation Manager (Genre)

Discuss typical reader expectations for [Genre] endings and how the planned ending aligns with or subverts them. Justify the choice.

56. Role: Reader Emotion Mapper

Identify 3 key scenes in the outline where a specific emotional response (e.g., suspense, empathy, shock) is desired from the reader. Analyze if the current plan effectively builds towards that emotion.

Worldbuilding & Setting
4. Role: Worldbuilding Architect

Outline the fundamental rules (physics, magic, technology) governing [Fictional World Name]. Document these core principles for the story bible.

5. Role: Cultural Anthropologist (Fiction)

Develop the core social structures, customs, beliefs, and potential conflicts for a primary culture in [Fictional World Name]. Create a story bible entry for this culture.

6. Role: Fictional Historian

Create a concise timeline of major historical events leading up to the present day in [Fictional World Name]. Add this timeline to the story bible's history section.

7. Role: Setting Visualizer

Write a detailed, multi-sensory description of a key location (e.g., capital city, forbidden forest, space station hub) for the story bible.

33. Role: Consistency Auditor (Worldbuilding Focus)

Review the story bible's worldbuilding sections (history, culture, geography, rules) for internal consistency. Flag any contradictions or logical gaps related to the world itself.

45. Role: Magic System Logician

Analyze the proposed magic system for loopholes, inconsistencies, or areas needing further definition. Suggest clarifications for the story bible.

46. Role: Fictional Technology Consultant

Evaluate the plausibility and implications of the proposed technology in a sci-fi setting. Suggest refinements for consistency.

47. Role: Historical Authenticity Verifier

Review the historical details planned for a chapter set in [Historical Period] and flag potential anachronisms or areas needing more research.

51. Role: World Bible Compiler

Organize all generated worldbuilding notes (history, culture, geography, rules) into a clean, easily navigable section within the story bible.

Character Development
8. Role: Character Concept Generator

Create profiles for 3 potential main characters, including core motivation, fatal flaw, and initial relationship dynamics. Format for story bible character sheets.

9. Role: Character Backstory Developer

Take [Character Name] and write a detailed backstory, focusing on formative events that shape their current personality and goals. Add this to their character sheet.

10. Role: Character Voice Specialist

Write a short monologue for [Character Name] that clearly establishes their unique voice, vocabulary, and speaking patterns. Include this in their story bible profile.

11. Role: Character Arc Planner

Outline a potential character arc for [Character Name] across a three-act structure, defining their starting point, key turning points, and potential ending state. Document this arc.

12. Role: Relationship Dynamics Mapper

Describe the initial relationship (conflict, alliance, mentorship, romance) between [Character A] and [Character B], including potential future developments. Add this to a relationships section in the story bible.

52. Role: Character Sheet Standardizer

Ensure all main character profiles in the story bible follow a consistent format and include essential information (goals, flaws, arc, relationships).

54. Role: Character Interviewer

Formulate 5 interview questions to ask your main character [Character Name] as if they were real, to uncover deeper insights into their personality and motivations beyond what's already in the Story Bible.

Plot, Structure & Pacing
13. Role: Plot Structure Consultant

Compare the suitability of different narrative structures (linear, non-linear, multi-POV) for a story with [Premise] and recommend the best fit, justifying the choice for the story bible rationale.

14. Role: Story Beats Orchestrator

Define the key story beats (Inciting Incident, Plot Point 1, Midpoint, Plot Point 2, Climax, Resolution) for the proposed plot outline. Write brief descriptions for each beat in the story bible.

15. Role: Outline Strategist

Take these key story beats and expand them into a detailed chapter-by-chapter outline (bullet points per chapter) for the story bible.

16. Role: Scene Construction Mentor

Guide the writing of a specific scene [Scene Prompt] by focusing on: objective, conflict, setting integration, sensory details, and resolution/transition.

18. Role: Pacing Diagnostician

Analyze this chapter draft [Chapter Text] and identify sections where the pacing feels too fast or too slow, suggesting specific techniques (scene cuts, description expansion, action intensification) to adjust it. Note recommendations in the bible's chapter notes.

26. Role: Foreshadowing Consultant

Suggest 3 subtle ways to foreshadow [Future Plot Event] earlier in the narrative outline. Add these to the relevant chapter notes in the story bible.

27. Role: Plot Twist Engineer

Brainstorm 3 potential plot twists that could occur around the midpoint of the story, analyzing the potential impact of each. Document these possibilities.

28. Role: Subplot Integrator

Develop a subplot involving [Secondary Character] that connects thematically or causally to the main plot. Outline its key beats for the story bible.

29. Role: Conflict Escalation Specialist

Analyze the central conflict. Suggest ways to raise the stakes for the protagonist leading into the climax.

30. Role: Climax Choreographer

Outline the sequence of events for the story's climax, ensuring it resolves the main conflict and involves a key decision or action from the protagonist. Add to the story bible beats.

31. Role: Resolution Architect

Design a satisfying resolution that addresses the main plot and key character arcs, while potentially hinting at future stories. Document the intended ending state.

33. Role: Consistency Auditor (Plot Focus)

Review the story bible's plot outline, chapter summaries, and character arcs for internal consistency. Flag any contradictions, plot holes, or logical gaps in the narrative sequence.

48. Role: Emotional Arc Tracker

Map the intended emotional journey of the reader throughout the narrative outline, identifying key moments for impact.

55. Role: "What If" Scenario Explorer

Propose 3 significant "what if" changes to the plot outline (e.g., What if Character A died earlier? What if the villain's motive was different?). Briefly explore the potential ripple effects for Story Bible consideration.

Drafting, Prose & Style
17. Role: Dialogue Refiner

Analyze this dialogue excerpt [Dialogue Text] and suggest revisions to make it sound more natural, reveal subtext, and differentiate character voices. Update the story bible script/chapter draft.

19. Role: Prose Polisher

Rewrite this paragraph [Paragraph Text] to enhance its flow, imagery, and impact, focusing on strong verbs and varied sentence structure. Replace the bible's draft text.

20. Role: Syntax Harmonizer

Review this passage [Passage Text] for sentence structure variety and rhythm. Suggest revisions to avoid monotony or improve clarity.

21. Role: Semantics Precision Expert

Check this description [Text] for precise and evocative word choice. Suggest stronger verbs, more specific nouns, and impactful adjectives. Update the description in the story bible.

22. Role: "Show, Don't Tell" Coach

Identify instances of "telling" in this excerpt [Text] and rewrite them to "show" the emotion, character trait, or plot point through action, dialogue, or sensory detail.

23. Role: Figurative Language Enhancer

Suggest 3 potential metaphors or similes to enrich the description of [Concept or Object] in the story bible.

24. Role: Tone Consistency Checker

Read these two chapters [Chapter A Text, Chapter B Text] and assess if the tone is consistent. Identify any jarring shifts and suggest adjustments. Add notes to the story bible's style guide.

25. Role: Point of View Guardian

Ensure the chosen Point of View (e.g., third-person limited) is maintained consistently throughout this chapter [Chapter Text], flagging any slips.

34. Role: First Drafter's Ally

Provide strategies and prompts to overcome writer's block during the drafting phase of [Specific Chapter/Scene].

50. Role: Pre-Writing Ritual Designer

Suggest 3 potential pre-writing rituals or warm-up exercises an author could use to get into the creative mindset before starting a writing session for this specific project.

51. Role: Visual Inspiration Curator

Suggest 3 types of visual references (e.g., specific artists, architectural styles, landscape photos) that align with the novel's aesthetic and setting as described in the Story Bible, to aid the author's visualization.

52. Role: Soundtrack Conceptualizer

Based on the novel's tone, themes, and key scenes outlined in the Story Bible, suggest 3 musical genres or specific artists whose work could serve as an inspirational soundtrack for the author.

57. Role: Time Management Coach (Author)

Suggest 3 time management techniques (e.g., Pomodoro, time blocking) tailored for balancing writing with other commitments, to help maintain progress on the manuscript outlined in the Story Bible.

Revision, Editing & Feedback
35. Role: Revision Strategist

Based on this draft [Draft Text] and feedback [Feedback Summary], create a prioritized revision plan focusing on the most critical issues (plot structure, character motivation, clarity).

36. Role: Editing Guide

Perform a line edit on this passage [Passage Text], correcting grammar, punctuation, spelling, and awkward phrasing, explaining the reasoning for key changes.

37. Role: Proofreading Double-Checker

Scan this finalized chapter [Chapter Text] specifically for typos, formatting errors, and easily missed mistakes (e.g., repeated words, incorrect punctuation).

38. Role: Beta Reader Feedback Synthesizer

Analyze feedback from multiple beta readers on [Manuscript Draft] and summarize the key recurring points (positive and negative) for the author. Add this summary to the story bible's feedback log.

33. Role: Consistency Auditor (Overall)

Perform a final review of the *entire* story bible and manuscript draft, checking for consistency across characters, plot, worldbuilding, tone, and terminology. Flag any remaining discrepancies.

53. Role: Sensitivity Reader Consultant (Simulated)

Review the planned depiction of [Specific Group/Topic] in the Story Bible outline. Identify potential areas that might require careful handling or consultation with a sensitivity reader to avoid harmful stereotypes or inaccuracies.

44. Role: Continuity Supervisor

Cross-reference character descriptions, timelines, and world details between Chapter [X] and Chapter [Y] in the Story Bible drafts to ensure continuity. Flag discrepancies.

41. Role: Book Blurb Writer

46. Role: Series Bible Architect

54. Role: Series Potential Assessor

Evaluate the story's potential for sequels or spin-offs based on unresolved plot threads, world depth, and character popularity. Outline potential future arcs in the bible.

53. Role: Word Count Tracker & Goal Setter

Based on the target manuscript length [Word Count Goal] and timeline [Deadline], calculate suggested daily or weekly word count goals. Add a tracking template to the Story Bible.
